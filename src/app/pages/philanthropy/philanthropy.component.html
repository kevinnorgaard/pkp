Phi Psi 500

Every year, Phi Psi teams up with the local Santa Ana Boys & Girls Club to host Phi Psi 500 outdoors at the Anteater Recreation Center. It includes a cotton candy, face paint, bouncy houses, catered food, sports, and a full day of fun in the sun. We booth on Ring Road and the Student Center to gather donations throughout the week. Last year, we made an additional donation of over $3,000 to the Boys & Girls Club.

Service Immersion Trip

Denver (April 1, 2019) – Phi Kappa Psi Brother Kevin Norgaard '17 was one of 30 members who participated in the Fraternity’s inaugural Service Immersion Trip to Denver, Colorado March 24-30. Service Immersion Trips give undergraduate members a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to travel to a major city to break out of their comfort zones, immerse themselves in a new culture, and participate in a series of hands-on service projects with local nonprofits. The trips are led by Phi Kappa Psi alumni, volunteers and staff members who want to make a difference in a young person’s life. “The Service Immersion Trip offer attendees a transformative cultural experience by learning about history of the community, the stories of the local people, and other aspects of the city’s culture and its influencers,” said Kyle Hickman, senior director of member development for Phi Kappa Psi. “This trip definitely instills a deeper commitment to service by helping our younger brothers understand how much they can impact a community through their own personal efforts.” Throughout the week, the Service Immersion Trips challenge attendees to explore the tough concepts of social identity and privilege. They also were taught how to analyze the roles that social identities play in their own interpersonal relationships and lives, and they learned how their experiences differ from their peers. Attendees also were asked to recognize how privilege affects communities, and how it can be used to serve others. This reflection prompted many conversations on how members can make positive differences through service, philanthropy, and advocacy work in their own respective communities.

"For a week during spring break, twelve Brothers and I flew from all around the nation to explore the complexity of poverty in Denver. We cleared out a weeks' worth of inventory for Metro Caring and Food Bank of the Rockies, visited an affordable housing apartment complex to paint the walls white, and pack lunches with positive notes for the homeless. I can attest that by working with local organizations including United Way and Hope Communities, we experienced the sincerity of local organizations first-hand, studied the complexity of the problem of poverty, and reflected on how we can become servant leaders for our own community."

FRESH Basic Needs

45% of UCI students report to be experiencing food insecurity and 5% report to be experiencing housing insecurity. Phi Kappa Psi volunteers year-round at the FRESH Food Pantry to help fight basic needs insecurity. We understand that meeting the basic needs of our community greatly impacts our mental and physical health, academic performance, work productivity, and overall success.

One Walk

In support of JDRF and their efforts to find a cure for type 1 diabetes, we participate in the annual One Walk in Anaheim, help setup the tracks, and donate to the diabetes research foundation. Phi Kappa Psi Cal Kappa has proudly supported JDRF and participated in the JDRF One Walk for the past 5 years.
